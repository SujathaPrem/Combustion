Combustion

What is combustion?

A chemical process in which a substance reacts with oxygen to give off heat is called combustion. The substance that undergoes combustion is said to be combustible. It is also called a fuel. The fuel may be solid, liquid or gas. Sometimes, light is also given off during combustion, either as a flame or as a glow.


Ignition Temperature

The lowest temperature at which a substance catches fire is called its ignition temperature.

How do you control fire?

When at all feasible, try to suffocate a fire by smothering it with the most suitable sort of fire extinguisher, water, or a covering substance like earth. A carbon dioxide extinguisher or a fire blanket can be used to remove oxygen from the area around the fire.

Types of Combustion

There are three types of combustion:
a) Rapid Combustion: It is a process in which a large amount of heat and light is released in a very short span of time.
b) Spontaneous Combustion: The type of combustion in which a material suddenly bursts into flames.
c) Explosion: A type of combustion during which a huge amount of heat and light is evolved with a sound, along with the production of gas.
